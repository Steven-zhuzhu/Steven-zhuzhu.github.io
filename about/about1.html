A Different Kind of Supernova: My Journey

I am drawn to astronomy, for in the universe’s boundless grandeur, I find my own worries vanishingly small.

I am equally drawn to physics, driven by the desire to peel back the surface of things and uncover the underlying laws that bind all matter.

Yet life is not all contemplation. On the badminton court, every smash fuels my spirit, gifting me a split-second boldness that echoes in all walks of life.

In quieter moments, I write poems and stage plays for children, trying to speak plain truths in simple words.

And when words do run out, I turn to my violin—music never struggles to express what language can not.

All these threads—the wonder, the logic, the release, and the expression—weave together into what I hold dearest: a commitment to supporting people with physical disabilities. It is how I pay forward the kindness I once received as a child while struggling with speech articulation difficulties.

Quantum Sonnet · 2024
In the void where particles dance and fade, I find a truth that cannot be betrayed— Not in the stars, nor in the earthly frame, But in the math that bears the cosmic name.
A wave, a particle, both and none, The universe hums to its own drum— We grasp at shadows, reach for light, Yet mystery is our constant night.
So I walk the line between the known and unknown, With equations as my compass, wonder as my throne— For in the quest to understand the sky, I find myself, and why I try.
