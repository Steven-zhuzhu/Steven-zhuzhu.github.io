A Different Kind of Supernova: My Journey

As a child with a speech disorder, I spent years piercing that barrier to find my voice.

I am drawn to astronomy, where the universe's boundless grandeur makes my own worries seem vanishingly small.

Physics, however, captivates me even more, driven by the desire to peel back the surface of things and uncover the underlying laws that bind all matter.

Yet I craft my world with my hands as with my mind. Every afternoon, my youth finds its voice on the badminton court. There, every smash fuels my spirit, gifting me a split-second boldness that echoes through all walks of life.

In quieter moments, I immerse myself in imaginary worlds, reading books and writing poems. I also write stage plays for children, striving to speak plain truths in simple words.

And when words run out, I turn to my violin—music never struggles to express what language can’t.

These passions converge, forming the elements of my universe as I strive to become my own kind of supernova.

Towards a Black Hole I Still Dream to Go · 2018

My Cosmic Dream
by Steven Zhu
Not to the Sahara's burning sand, Nor Antarctica's icy stand; The lost Atlantis' ancient glow, Or Mars' life that we don't know, But toward a black hole I long to go.
As an astronaut, I take my flight, Toward the central core, where the stars shine bright; Past super-planets, massive and grand, Soon to be consumed by the black hole's command.
I see a hot ice planet, a frozen delight, But gravity pulls me away from its sight; Then a white dwarf star, with a heat-absorbing might, Explodes into a super-planet, launching me in flight.
Into the black hole, I take a dive, Feeling the sun's rays, as if alive; But got soon engulfed, spin in strife, Witnessing the universe's heart, full of fabled life.
With the most massive gravity, it reigns supreme, Infinite matter, in space so extreme; Unfathomable size, yet small like a dream, Even light cannot escape its beam.
From a behemoth of the cosmos, a dragon of old, I pull out a snowball, so tiny, yet bold Ten times Earth's weight, instantly swallowed.
In space's vastness, I twist and twirl, uncontrolled.
But then I suddenly awaken, From my bed, my dreams are shaken; "Don't daydream now, it's time for school," I realize, alas, my adventure's only a fool's tool.
