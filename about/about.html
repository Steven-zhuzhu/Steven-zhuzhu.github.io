A Different Kind of Supernova: My Journey

I am drawn to astronomy, for in the universe’s boundless grandeur, I find my own worries vanishingly small.

I am equally drawn to physics, driven by the desire to peel back the surface of things and uncover the underlying laws that bind all matter.

Yet life is not all contemplation. On the badminton court, every smash fuels my spirit, gifting me a split-second boldness that echoes in all walks of life.

In quieter moments, I immerse myself in imaginary worlds, reading books and write poems. I also write stage plays for children, trying to speak plain truths in simple words.

And when words run out, I turn to my violin—music never struggles to express what language cannot.

These passions converge, forming the very elements of my universe as I strive to become my own kind of supernova.

My Quantum Inspiration

In the void where particles dance and fade, I find a truth that cannot be betrayed— Not in the stars, nor in the earthly frame, But in the math that bears the cosmic name.
A wave, a particle, both and none, The universe hums to its own drum— We grasp at shadows, reach for light, Yet mystery is our constant night.
So I walk the line between the known and unknown, With equations as my compass, wonder as my throne— For in the quest to understand the sky, I find myself, and why I try.

Towards a Black Hole I Still Dream to Go · 2018

My Cosmic Dream
by Steven Zhu
Not to the Sahara's burning sand, Nor Antarctica's icy stand; The lost Atlantis' ancient glow, Or Mars' life that we don't know, But toward a black hole I long to go.
As an astronaut, I take my flight, Toward the central core, where the stars shine bright; Past super-planets, massive and grand, Soon to be consumed by the black hole's command.
I see a hot ice planet, a frozen delight, But gravity pulls me away from its sight; Then a white dwarf star, with a heat-absorbing might, Explodes into a super-planet, launching me in flight.
Into the black hole, I take a dive, Feeling the sun's rays, as if alive; But got soon engulfed, spin in strife, Witnessing the universe's heart, full of fabled life.
With the most massive gravity, it reigns supreme, Infinite matter, in space so extreme; Unfathomable size, yet small like a dream, Even light cannot escape its beam.
From a behemoth of the cosmos, a dragon of old, I pull out a snowball, so tiny, yet bold Ten times Earth's weight, instantly swallowed.
In space's vastness, I twist and twirl, uncontrolled.
But then I suddenly awaken, From my bed, my dreams are shaken; "Don't daydream now, it's time for school," I realize, alas, my adventure's only a fool's tool.
