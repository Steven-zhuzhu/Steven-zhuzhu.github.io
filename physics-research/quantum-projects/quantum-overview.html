Quantum Mechanics Projects

Overview of Quantum Mechanics Research

Quantum mechanics is the core direction of my physics research, focusing on simulating and verifying basic quantum mechanical models through theoretical derivation and programming implementation. These projects lay a solid foundation for in-depth research on quantum computing and quantum information in the future.

All projects are implemented based on Python, including NumPy for numerical calculations and Matplotlib for visualization, with complete reproducibility and extensibility.

Project 1: Particle in a One-Dimensional Box

A classic quantum mechanical model that studies the energy levels and wave functions of particles confined in a one-dimensional infinite potential well. This project systematically explores the differences between quantum and classical mechanics.

  • Theoretical derivation of energy eigenvalues and normalized wave functions
  • Numerical simulation of probability density distribution at different energy levels
  • Visualization of wave function superposition and boundary conditions
  • Comparison of classical and quantum probability distribution characteristics

Project 2: Quantum Tunneling Effect Simulation

Research on the non-classical phenomenon where quantum particles can pass through potential barriers higher than their energy, which is the physical basis of many modern technologies (e.g., scanning tunneling microscopes, semiconductor devices).

  • Derivation of transmission/reflection coefficients for rectangular potential barriers
  • Python-based numerical solution of the time-independent Schr√∂dinger equation
  • Visualization of wave function penetration depth and tunneling probability
  • Analysis of tunneling effect in practical quantum devices

Key Research Outcomes & Future Directions

Through these quantum projects, I have mastered the basic mathematical tools of quantum mechanics and the ability to combine theoretical physics with programming practice. Future research will focus on:

  • Extending to two/three-dimensional quantum well models
  • Exploring the application of quantum tunneling in quantum computing
  • Combining machine learning with quantum mechanical simulations
