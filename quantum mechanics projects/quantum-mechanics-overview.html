Quantum Mechanics Projects - Overview

Abstract (Overall)

This series of quantum mechanics projects aims to demystify core quantum phenomena through computational modeling and interactive visualization. By translating abstract quantum equations (Schrödinger equation, wavefunctions, tunneling) into intuitive visual outputs (plots, animations), the projects bridge the gap between theoretical quantum physics and practical understanding. The work focuses on two foundational quantum models (particle in a box, quantum tunneling) and the computational tools developed to visualize them, serving as an educational resource for students and enthusiasts new to quantum mechanics.

Project Series

Project 1: Quantum Particle in a Box

Core Intro: A visualization of the simplest quantum bound state model, exploring discrete energy levels, wavefunctions, and probability densities. Key focus: classical vs. quantum behavior in confined spaces. This project breaks down the foundational "particle in a box" model into interactive plots, making quantized energy levels tangible for learners.

Project 2: Quantum Tunneling

Core Intro: An exploration of the counterintuitive quantum effect where particles penetrate potential barriers despite insufficient energy. Includes real-world applications (STM, radioactive decay) and transmission probability calculations. This project explains why quantum particles can "tunnel" through barriers that classical particles cannot cross.

Research Background

Quantum mechanics is a cornerstone of modern physics, yet its counterintuitive principles often pose challenges for learners. These projects leverage computational physics to make quantum phenomena tangible—through interactive visualizations, students can observe how wavefunctions evolve, how particles tunnel through barriers, and how quantum behavior diverges from classical physics. The codebase is open-source and well-documented, designed to be adaptable for classroom use or self-directed learning.
