My Childhood Audio Book

By the time I was around nine years old, others could finally understand what I said—and a new world was slowly opening its doors to me. At that time, I had just moved back to China from Canada with my mother. While my Chinese classmates struggled with their English studies every single day, I wanted to share my reads and build them a reading ladder. I hoped to help them just as my mother had loved and helped me back then, and pass my own love on to them. I launched a reading column on Ximalaya Audio in China, reading books aloud for them, and my classmates became my first little audience. Listening to those recordings now, my voice sounds childish and my pronunciations are a bit stumbling—I wasn’t that good at it, for I was still fighting against my dysarthria. To this day, these audio books still lie there quietly, accessible for listening anytime. Here is the link to my Ximalaya channel: https://www.ximalaya.com/zhubo/46168181
