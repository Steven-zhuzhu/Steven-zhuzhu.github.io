Violin, Amateur (2017- 2024)

I believe music has a magical power to heal and articulate deep emotions. Playing the violin in my spare time is a profound source of joy and expression for me, a way to connect with emotions that are often hard to put into words.

"Music is the universal language of mankind." – Henry Wadsworth Longfellow

My Musical Journey - The Learning Challenge

I have played the violin for about eight years. It's never easy to master any musical instrument, and the violin is even more challenging. Unlike the piano, it has no fixed pitch or tone; the timbre, pitch and rhythm of every note all rely on precise control with both hands.

In those quiet afternoons filled with joy, or evenings clouded by sorrow and stuck with unsolvable problems, the violin was my go-to emotional outlet. It never failed to calm my mind and let me express all my feelings freely without words.

My Musical Journey - Orchestra Experience

A key and precious part of this musical journey was performing on stage with the Shenzhen Poly International Youth String Orchestra. Through consistent daily practice and unremitting polishing of my skills, I worked my way up to become the first violinist of the orchestra.

In every performance, I sit next to the conductor, leading the playing of the first violin section of the entire orchestra. This experience not only honed my technical skills but also deepened my understanding of music – teaching me the importance of teamwork, timing, and musical expression in a collaborative setting.

My Favorite Violin Piece

My favorite violin piece for appreciation is Zigeunerweisen (Gypsy Airs) by Sarasate — a classic pinnacle among violin pieces. This piece perfectly blends the sorrow and passion of Gypsy culture, with a graceful and melodious melody and full of timbre expressiveness.

Its rich emotional layers make me deeply intoxicated and truly feel the unique artistic charm of the violin. Because of its extraordinary artistic value and technical complexity, I have always regarded it as a classic appreciation piece that represents the beauty of violin music.

Listen to Zigeunerweisen

Although I can only appreciate the beauty of this piece from a spectator's perspective for now and have not yet reached the level to play it independently, this does not dilute my love for it at all. Instead, it becomes a constant motivation for me to continue improving my violin skills and pursuing musical excellence.

Musical Inspiration & Future Pursuit

Eight years of violin learning have not only given me a valuable skill but also shaped my way of thinking and emotional expression. The discipline required for daily practice has translated into perseverance in my academic and research pursuits, while the artistic sensibility from music has brought a unique perspective to my study of physics and other sciences.

Music and science may seem like two different worlds, but they both share the same pursuit of beauty, order and harmony. For the future, I will continue to keep the violin as my lifelong hobby, using it to balance the rigor of academic research and to nourish my soul. I look forward to exploring more classic violin pieces and possibly collaborating with other musicians to create and perform in the future.
